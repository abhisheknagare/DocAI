ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Apple Inc. (the "Company," "Apple") designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories. Apple also sells a variety of related services. The Company's fiscal year is the 52 or 53-week period that ends on the last Saturday of September.

Apple is committed to bringing the best user experience to its customers through its innovative hardware, software, and services. Apple's products and services include iPhone, Mac, iPad, Apple Watch, AirPods, Apple TV, HomePod, and a portfolio of consumer and professional software applications.

PRODUCTS

iPhone is Apple's line of smartphones based on its iOS operating system. iPhone designs incorporate motion sensors, cameras, displays, and cellular network capabilities. In fiscal 2024, iPhone net sales were $201.0 billion, representing approximately 52% of total net sales, compared to $205.5 billion in fiscal 2023.

Mac includes desktops and notebooks based on Apple silicon. Mac net sales were $29.8 billion in fiscal 2024, compared to $29.4 billion in fiscal 2023.

iPad includes iPad Pro, iPad Air, iPad, and iPad mini. iPad net sales were $26.7 billion in fiscal 2024, compared to $28.3 billion in fiscal 2023.

Wearables, Home and Accessories includes Apple Watch, AirPods, Beats products, Apple TV, and accessories. Net sales were $37.0 billion in fiscal 2024, compared to $39.8 billion in fiscal 2023.

Services include advertising, AppleCare, cloud services, digital content, payment, and licensing. Services net sales were $96.2 billion in fiscal 2024, compared to $85.2 billion in fiscal 2023 — a growth of approximately 13%.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

The following discussion of risk factors contains forward-looking statements. These risk factors may be important to understanding any statement in this Form 10-K or elsewhere.

Macroeconomic Risks: The Company's business and financial condition are sensitive to global and regional macroeconomic conditions, including inflation, interest rates, and consumer spending trends. Uncertain or weak economic conditions may harm the Company's business and results of operations.

Competition Risk: The markets for the Company's products and services are highly competitive, and the Company is subject to intense competition in all areas of its business. The Company faces competition from vertically integrated competitors that may have better access to key components, lower cost structures, or alternative revenue models.

Supply Chain Risk: The Company has outsourced manufacturing to third parties. Global supply chain disruptions, including those arising from natural disasters, geopolitical tensions, or pandemics, could adversely impact production and availability of the Company's products.

Intellectual Property Risk: The Company could be found to have infringed the intellectual property rights of others. The Company regularly receives notices from third parties alleging patent or other intellectual property infringement.

Regulatory Risk: The Company is subject to complex and changing laws and regulations worldwide, including those related to privacy, data protection, content, antitrust, and tax. Compliance with these requirements creates significant costs and may reduce the Company's flexibility.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Fiscal Year 2024 Highlights

Total net sales for fiscal 2024 were $391.0 billion, compared to $383.3 billion in fiscal 2023, an increase of approximately 2%. This growth was primarily driven by strong performance in our Services segment.

Total operating expenses for fiscal 2024 were $53.9 billion, compared to $54.8 billion in fiscal 2023.

Net income for fiscal 2024 was $93.7 billion, compared to $97.0 billion in fiscal 2023. Diluted earnings per share were $6.08, compared to $6.13 in fiscal 2023.

Gross margin for fiscal 2024 was $180.7 billion or 46.2%, compared to $169.1 billion or 44.1% in fiscal 2023. The increase in gross margin percentage was primarily due to an improved mix driven by Services growth and favorable product mix.

LIQUIDITY AND CAPITAL RESOURCES

The Company believes its existing balances of cash and cash equivalents and short-term investments, together with cash generated by operations and access to credit markets, will be sufficient to satisfy its cash requirements and capital return program over the next twelve months.

Cash and cash equivalents and current and non-current marketable securities totaled $153.2 billion as of September 28, 2024, compared to $162.1 billion as of September 30, 2023.

During fiscal 2024, the Company returned $110.0 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases. The Company also paid dividends of $0.96 per share during fiscal 2024.

ITEM 1A. ADDITIONAL RISK FACTORS

Cybersecurity Risk: The Company's products, services, and business operations are subject to cybersecurity threats including malware, ransomware, and sophisticated state-sponsored attacks that could disrupt operations or expose sensitive customer data.

Geopolitical Risk: The Company's operations in China are subject to significant political, regulatory, and economic risks. China represents approximately 17% of net sales. Restrictions on trade, travel bans, or regulatory actions by Chinese authorities could materially impact revenues.
